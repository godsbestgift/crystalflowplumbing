# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Homeowners in Berks, Lebanon & Lancaster Counties (PA) with an active plumbing/drain problem — slow drains, sewer smell, gurgling pipes, standing water, backups. They're often mid-crisis (urgent, stressed, price-anxious) and comparing local providers fast. Job to be done: find a trustworthy, fairly-priced local plumber and call now.

## Product Purpose

Crystal Flow Repair & Drain Cleaning's marketing site. Drives phone calls / free-quote requests for drain cleaning and repair services. Success = visitor picks up the phone or submits a quote request within the first screen or two, without needing to dig for pricing or trust signals.

## Brand Personality

Trustworthy, direct, local — a father-son owned trades business, not a franchise or faceless service. Executed with a polished, premium visual layer (ice-blue glow accents, chrome borders, dark navy + gold) rather than a bare-bones local-biz look. Confidence without corporate gloss; "the owner answers the phone" is a core promise, reflected in copy and trust chips.

## Anti-references

- Generic SaaS/tech startup landing page (hero-metric templates, gradient text, cookie-cutter feature-card grids) — this is a home-service trades brand, not software.
- Cheap/DIY local-plumber template look (stock clipart, harsh primary colors, cluttered layout) — the polish is intentional and should stay.

## Design Principles

1. **Owner-direct trust over corporate distance** — every CTA and trust signal should reinforce "you're calling a real person who owns this," not a call center.
2. **Price clarity first** — visitors are price-anxious; upfront/clear pricing language beats vague reassurance.
3. **Urgency without panic** — copy speaks to real problems (slow drain, sewer smell) plainly, without fear-mongering.
4. **One clear next action per screen** — call or get a quote; don't dilute with competing CTAs (flagged CTA-copy audit already queued in project memory).
5. **Premium local, not corporate-generic** — the ice-blue/navy/gold system is the brand's own; don't drift toward generic SaaS aesthetics when adding new sections.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

No formal WCAG level set yet. Given the crisis/urgency context (older homeowners, stressed users), prioritize large touch targets (mobile sticky bar already at 54px min-height), high body-text contrast, and no motion that blocks reading. Treat as WCAG AA baseline until told otherwise.
